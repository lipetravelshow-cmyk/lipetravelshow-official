4 things I'd compare before buying

Price alone almost never tells the full story. The ideal is to look at cost alongside time, effort and how well it fits your actual trip.

Nearby dates

Sometimes one or two days make a big difference in price and comfort. It's worth testing flexible dates before committing.

Total travel time

The cheapest flight isn't always the smartest — when the journey gets too long or fragmented, the real cost rises in energy and logistics.

Connections and schedules

A good connection can work — a bad one can drain your trip right from the start. Check layover times and airports before confirming.

Route logic

Think about whether the departure, return and chosen airport fit the rest of your itinerary — or create unnecessary detours.

What I'd avoid

Looking only at the lowest price without checking total travel time. Choosing a bad connection to save a little. Researching hotels before understanding the flight logic. Booking without checking baggage rules and final schedules.

How I research in practice

Before buying, I compare nearby dates, total travel times, connections and how well the route fits the rhythm of the trip. That avoids rushed decisions.
